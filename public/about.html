About Me

Hi, I'm Rose — Nguyen Thi Phuong Hong. I'm a Technical Product Manager with over 14 years of hands-on experience, building software applications, web apps, and websites that real users love.

My journey through product hasn't been a straight line — and that's exactly what makes me effective. I started as a Business Analyst, evolved into a Scrum Master, grew into a Product Owner, and now lead as a Technical Product Manager. Each role taught me a different lens: how to listen to the business, how to unblock a team, how to own outcomes, and how to bridge the gap between what stakeholders ask for and what engineers can actually build.

What I bring to the table

I speak both languages — business and engineering. I can sit with a customer in the morning to unpack a vague pain point, then sit with developers in the afternoon to debate API contracts and database schemas. I write code in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, SQL, C++, and Python — not to replace my engineers, but to understand them, challenge them constructively, and never be the bottleneck in a technical conversation.

I'm the person who writes the Design Solution Documents, Functional Requirement Specifications (FRS), and user stories that teams actually want to read — clear, testable, and grounded in real user needs. I genuinely enjoy working directly with clients: discovery sessions, requirement workshops, demos, tough conversations about scope. That's where products are won or lost, and it's my favorite part of the job that I love most.

What I've delivered

Across 14+ years, I've shipped products across web platforms, mobile applications, and enterprise software. I've led cross-functional teams through full product lifecycles — from discovery and roadmapping to launch and iteration. I've worked in Agile/Scrum environments, facilitated ceremonies, managed backlogs, and translated ambiguous business goals into measurable product outcomes.

A personal note — and why I'm here

I used to dream big. I dreamed of traveling across Europe and Australia, of working with clients and companies in those parts of the world. Life had other plans — I'm now a proud mother of two, and that dream is, for now, gently on hold. I won't pretend that doesn't sting a little.

But here's the thing: becoming a mother didn't shrink my ambition — it sharpened it. I'm now looking for remote, flexible roles where I can be fully present for my kids and deliver the kind of focused, senior-level product work I've spent over a decade refining. Remote work isn't a compromise for me — it's the format where I do my best thinking. No commute, no performative busywork, just outcomes.

If you're a company in Europe or Australia building something meaningful in software, web, or mobile — and you value senior product talent who can write code, talk to customers, and ship — let's talk. My dream didn't disappear. It just learned to work across time zones.
